Essential Functions

Certain core functions are essential for cellular viability and must be present in all bacterial organisms. The minimal bacterial genome required for viability (Syn1.0) was used as a basis to determine 'essential' functions. Essential genes were manually mapped to GO terms and classified into general functional categories.

Pathways

Many biological processes require the coordinated action of multiple proteins. Predictions should ensure that all necessary components of critical pathways are identified. Pathway and process completeness were evaluated using both MetaCyc pathway information and the 'has_part' relations in the GO.

Spatial Consistency

Cellular processes are often compartmentalized within specific organelles or cellular regions. The Gene Ontology (GO) 'occurs_in' relation to specify the cellular component in which a molecular function or biological process takes place, so it's used to evaluate spatial consistency.
